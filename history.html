History

The great man Sankardev started the culture of Assamese drama in Assam with his drama “Chinhayatra”. The addition of Gunaviram Baruah’s “Ramnavami” in the middle part of 1982 century, the drama movement got a momentum in Assam. In this way people continued the development of drama movement in Assam since then. With a view to develop this movement the drama organization ‘Sadou Asom Akanka Nat Sanmilan’ got birth in Dibrugrah town under the presidentship of Kalaguru Bishnu Prasad Rabha and the Sanmilon continued its activities in the field of drama. After a few years in the convention held in Mangaldoi in 1967, the constitution was amended and the organisation was renamed as “Asom Natya Sanmilan.” Since then the organisation took step to open its branches in the different parts of Assam. Many big and profound personalities like Bishnu Prasad Rabha, Prafulla Kumar Baruah, Phunu Baruah, Krishna Prasad Baruah, Lakshyadhar Choudhury, Phani Talukdar, Arun Sharma Dr. Akhil Chakraborty, Dr. Yogendra Chetia, Abdul Mazid and many more decorated the presidential chair of the Asom Natya Sanmilan acknowledged as the national organization of Assam and created the environment for further development of drama culture in Assam. Asom Natya Sanmilan had organized one act drama competition from 1959 to 1967. It started full drama (90 Minutes) competition in the year 1977 in place of one act drama. This step contributed immensely towards the creation of many renowned workers in the field of drama and many ideal and instructive drama’s. Asom Natya Sanmilan opened 14th District committees, four Mandals (zones) - Ratnapith, Kampith, Swarnapith and Soumarpith and many branches for facilitating the implementation of its agenda in the field of drama. Since its inception Asom Natya Sanmilan has been observing 12th September as “Silpi Samaroh Divas” in the memory of Natya Silpi Brajnath Sarmah. In the memory of this great personality in the field of drama the Brajnath Sharma Award has been forwarded to the person helping the development of drama culture in Assam since 2010 every year. Asom Natya Sanmilan has been organizing one act drama competition since 2005 and it has already completed its successful 8 years under the sponsorship of Oil India Ltd. Under the editorship of famous dramatist Arun Sharma a collection of selected dramas covering one and half century from 1855 to 1905 and including Gunaviram Baruah’a “Ramnavami” has already been published and the process for the publication of 2nd part is underway. The construction work of permanent office of state committee of Asom Natya Sanmilan is in progress in land area of 8 bighas and 1 katha at Birah Bebejiya in Nagaon. The branches of Asom Natya Sanmilan have been, time to time, organizing drama workshop, Recitation workshop, drama writing workshop, Janagosthiya Natya Samaroh, Child drama workshop etc. and trying their level best to give momentum to the drama in movement in Assam.
